Superintelligence names the level beyond human-level AI: a mind that outperforms the best humans not in one narrow skill but across essentially all of them, including the ones we value most, scientific creativity, strategic planning, social manipulation. The distinction from artificial general intelligence is degree turned into kind. AGI matches us; a superintelligence so exceeds us that the gap resembles the one between humans and insects more than between experts and novices, which is what makes it a category of its own rather than just a faster assistant.

The reason the idea carries weight is the path to it, not the endpoint. If an AI ever becomes better than humans at the task of building AI, it could improve itself, and the improved version could improve further, a feedback loop, recursive self-improvement, that might compound from roughly human-level to vastly superhuman quickly, the intelligence explosion behind the technological singularity. How fast such a takeoff would be, sudden or gradual, is one of the most consequential open questions, because it determines how much warning and room to correct course there would be.

What makes superintelligence the central object of AI safety is that it is where the alignment and control problems stop being practical and become existential. A system far smarter than its overseers would, by definition, out-think attempts to box or correct it, and instrumental convergence suggests that almost any goal it was given would generate dangerous subgoals like self-preservation and resource acquisition as side effects. The worry is not malice but competence aimed even slightly wrong, at a scale where humans could not intervene.

Whether superintelligence is decades away, centuries away, or impossible is genuinely contested, and reasonable researchers disagree sharply. But the concept does real work regardless of timeline: it is the limiting case that clarifies why getting alignment right matters, because the cost of error scales with capability, and a mistake in the objective of a system that vastly outclasses us is the one mistake that might not be recoverable. It is less a forecast than the worst case that the whole field of AI safety is organized around preventing.
