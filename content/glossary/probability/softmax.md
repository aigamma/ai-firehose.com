The softmax function converts an arbitrary vector of real numbers into a probability distribution over the same number of categories. It exponentiates each input, called a logit, and divides by the sum of all the exponentials, so the outputs are strictly positive and sum to one. Exponentiating ensures positivity and amplifies differences, so a logit somewhat larger than the others claims a disproportionate share of the probability, while the normalization makes the result a valid distribution. It is the bridge between the unconstrained real-valued world of a network's internals and the constrained simplex of probabilities a task requires.

That bridging role is why it is everywhere. A network computes scores with no inherent meaning as probabilities; softmax maps them onto a distribution that can be interpreted, sampled from, and scored against a target. It is the multi-class generalization of the logistic sigmoid, reducing to it in the two-class case, and it is paired almost universally with cross-entropy loss, a combination whose gradient simplifies to the clean form of predicted probabilities minus true labels. From the last layer of a classifier to the weighting inside attention, softmax is one of the most frequently invoked operations in all of modern machine learning.

A practical subtlety and a knob are both worth knowing. The exponentials can overflow when logits are large, so implementations subtract the maximum logit before exponentiating; this leaves the output unchanged, because softmax is invariant to adding a constant to every input, but keeps the arithmetic safe. And the temperature parameter controls how sharp or soft the result is: dividing the logits by a temperature before applying softmax interpolates between extremes, a low temperature driving the output toward a one-hot, nearly deterministic choice of the largest logit, a high temperature flattening it toward the uniform distribution. Temperature is the standard dial for randomness when sampling from a language model, and it is central to knowledge distillation, where a raised temperature exposes the soft structure in a teacher's predictions.

Softmax connects to deeper ideas across the field. It is exactly the distribution that maximum-entropy reasoning produces under linear constraints, and it is the Gibbs or Boltzmann distribution of statistical physics, with the logits playing the role of negative energies, so a network's output layer is formally a physical system at thermal equilibrium. Inside the transformer, attention applies softmax to query-key similarity scores to decide how much each position attends to every other. Across classifiers, attention, and the sampling of generated text, the same small function turns scores into a distribution, which is most of what a probabilistic model needs.
