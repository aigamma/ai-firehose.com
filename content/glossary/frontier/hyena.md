Hyena is a sequence mixing operator built as a drop-in alternative to self-attention that avoids its quadratic cost. It is made of two ingredients applied in alternation: very long convolutions, whose filters span the entire sequence, and element-wise multiplicative gating, where the signal is modulated by a projection of the input itself. Stacking several of these convolution-then-gate steps gives the Hyena operator: the long convolutions let information travel across the whole sequence in one layer, supplying the global receptive field attention provides, while the gating introduces a data-dependent, multiplicative interaction a plain convolution lacks.

The defining technical move is that the convolution filters are not stored as explicit weight vectors. A filter as long as the sequence would be enormous and would tie the model to a fixed maximum length; instead Hyena parameterizes each filter implicitly, generating its values from a small neural network that maps a position to a filter weight. This keeps the parameter count modest and independent of sequence length, lets the same model run at different lengths, and produces smooth filters that generalize well, and the long convolutions themselves are evaluated efficiently with the fast Fourier transform, which computes a convolution in time that grows as the length times its logarithm rather than as the length squared.

Hyena showed that a carefully built convolutional operator, with no attention at all, can approach the language-modeling quality of transformers while scaling much better to long contexts, and the telling result is which ingredient did the work: the gating is essential. Experiments showed that long convolutions alone fell short, and that the multiplicative, input-controlled interaction was what recovered the expressivity needed to match attention. As context lengths grow into the tens of thousands of tokens, Hyena's near-linear scaling opens a regime quadratic attention cannot reach economically, which made it influential in long-sequence domains including genomics.

Hyena is a close cousin of the state space model family, and the relationship is direct: a structured state space layer also realizes a long convolution, just one whose kernel is generated by a linear recurrence rather than by a position network, so S4 and Hyena are two ways of building the same long-convolution primitive. Both stand alongside linear attention and the retentive network as members of the broader class of efficient sequence mixers seeking to replace all-pairs attention, and the gating idea at Hyena's heart reappears throughout that class, including in the selective mechanism of mamba.
