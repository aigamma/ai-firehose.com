Automatic differentiation is the machinery that lets a computer compute exact derivatives of any function written as a program, with no human deriving formulas and no approximation error. It rests on a simple observation: every program, however elaborate, is ultimately a composition of elementary operations, additions, multiplications, exponentials, and the like, whose individual derivatives are known. By recording the sequence of these operations as a computation graph and applying the chain rule systematically across them, automatic differentiation assembles the derivative of the whole from the derivatives of the parts. The result is exact, correct to machine precision, and automatic, requiring no manual calculus.

It is worth distinguishing it sharply from the two methods it replaces, because it is often confused with both. Symbolic differentiation manipulates algebraic expressions to produce a formula for the derivative, but the formulas explode in size for deep compositions and demand the function be written as a closed-form expression rather than arbitrary code with loops and branches. Numerical differentiation approximates the derivative by evaluating the function at nearby points and taking a difference quotient, which is easy but suffers from truncation and rounding error and costs one extra evaluation per input dimension. Automatic differentiation sidesteps both: exact derivatives, ordinary program control flow, and whole gradients computed efficiently.

It comes in two modes that differ in the order of multiplication along the chain. Forward mode propagates derivatives from inputs toward outputs and is efficient when there are few inputs and many outputs. Reverse mode propagates from outputs back toward inputs and is efficient in the opposite case, many inputs and one output, which is exactly the shape of deep learning, where a single scalar loss depends on billions of parameters. Reverse-mode automatic differentiation applied to a neural network is precisely backpropagation: a forward pass records the intermediate values and the graph, and a backward pass walks it in reverse, accumulating the gradient of the loss with respect to every parameter by multiplying through each operation's local Jacobian.

Automatic differentiation is the foundation on which modern deep learning frameworks are built; it is the autograd engine inside PyTorch, JAX, and TensorFlow. By making the gradient of any model available essentially for free once the forward computation is specified, it freed researchers from the error-prone labor of deriving and coding gradients by hand and let them design arbitrarily complex architectures, confident the framework will differentiate them correctly. Every step of gradient descent on a deep network consumes a gradient produced this way, which is why automatic differentiation, though it runs silently beneath the abstractions, is one of the genuine enabling technologies of the field.
